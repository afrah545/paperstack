How to Add a Watermark to a PDF for Free

5 min read · Updated June 2026

You're sending out a draft, a proposal, or a sample document and want to mark it clearly as not-final — or you're sharing your own work and want your name or logo visibly on every page before it goes out into the world. A watermark does both jobs: it's a quick visual flag stamped across the page without altering the actual content underneath.

When you'd actually need this

  • Draft documents — marking a proposal or contract clearly as "DRAFT" before it's finalized
  • Branding your own work — adding your name, logo, or business name across a portfolio or sample

Getting the look right

  • Keep opacity low for text-heavy documents — a watermark at 15-25% opacity is visible without making the underlying text hard to read.
  • Diagonal placement reads as more deliberate — a watermark angled across the page (rather than straight horizontal) is the more common convention for "draft" or "confidential" markings.
  • Logo watermarks work best with transparency — a PNG with a transparent background looks far cleaner than a logo with a solid white box behind it.
💡 Quick tip If the watermark is meant purely as a visual deterrent against unauthorized reuse rather than for readability, a slightly higher opacity makes it harder to crop or edit out of the page.

Common questions

Q.Can I remove a watermark once it's added?
Not with this tool, and that's intentional — a watermark meant to mark a document as a draft or protect its origin shouldn't be trivially removable. If you need a clean version later, keep your original unwatermarked file saved separately.
Q.Will the watermark appear on every page?
Yes, by default it applies across all pages, keeping the marking consistent throughout the document.
Q.Can I use a colored watermark instead of plain gray?
Yes — pick any text color, or upload a full-color logo image if you're using an image-based watermark.
Q.Is it safe to watermark a sensitive document using this tool?
Yes — PaperStack adds the watermark entirely inside your browser. Your file is never uploaded to a server.
