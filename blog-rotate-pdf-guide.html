How to Rotate a PDF for Free (Fix Sideways Pages)

5 min read · Updated June 2026

You open a PDF and one page — sometimes just one, sometimes all of them — is sideways or upside down. It usually happens after scanning a document that was fed into the scanner the wrong way, or after combining pages from different sources. Reading a sideways page by tilting your head or your laptop screen isn't a real fix.

Rotating a PDF takes a few seconds and doesn't require re-scanning or re-printing anything. Here's how.

When you'd actually need this

  • Scanned documents — pages fed into a scanner in the wrong orientation
  • Mixed-source merges — combining files where some pages were originally landscape and others portrait
  • Phone photos of documents — a photo taken sideways that didn't auto-correct

Rotating just one page in a multi-page file

You don't need to rotate the whole document if only one or two pages are affected. Most rotate tools, including this one, let you select individual pages from the thumbnail view and rotate only those — leaving correctly-oriented pages untouched.

💡 Quick tip If a page still looks wrong after rotating, try rotating it a second time in the same direction — a single rotation moves 90°, so a fully upside-down page needs two rotations to come back around.

Common questions

Q.Will rotating affect the text or image quality?
No — rotation only changes how the page is displayed, not its content. Nothing is recompressed or altered.
Q.Can I rotate different pages in different directions?
Yes — select pages individually rather than rotating the whole document, if different pages need to go different ways.
Q.Why does the page look fine on my phone but sideways on my laptop?
Some PDF viewers auto-rotate pages for display based on saved metadata, while others don't. Rotating the actual file fixes it consistently across every viewer and every device.
Q.Is it safe to rotate a PDF with sensitive content?
Yes — PaperStack rotates your file entirely inside your browser. It's never uploaded to a server.
