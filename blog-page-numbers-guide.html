How to Add Page Numbers to a PDF for Free

5 min read · Updated June 2026

You've merged a few documents into one PDF, or you're finishing a report, thesis, or proposal, and now it needs page numbers — but the document you started from (or the merge you just did) doesn't have them. Manually typing numbers onto each page isn't realistic for anything beyond a couple of pages.

When you'd actually need this

  • Reports and proposals — page numbers make a multi-page document easier to navigate and reference
  • Academic work — theses, dissertations, and assignments almost always require numbered pages
  • Contracts and legal documents — numbered pages make it easy to reference "see page 4, clause 2"
  • After merging files — a merged PDF rarely carries over consistent numbering from its source files

Where to place numbers, depending on the document

  • Reports and business documents — bottom-center or bottom-right is the standard convention.
  • Academic papers — check your institution's formatting guide first; many require a specific corner and font size.
  • Documents with a cover page — most conventions start numbering from the first content page, not the cover. If your tool numbers every page including the cover, consider starting the sequence at "0" for the cover or excluding it depending on what's required.
💡 Quick tip If you'll be merging more pages into this document later, add page numbers as the very last step — numbering before merging means you'll likely end up with duplicate or out-of-order numbers across the combined file.

Common questions

Q.Can I start numbering from something other than 1?
Yes — set a custom starting number, which is useful if this PDF is one section of a larger numbered document.
Q.Can I skip numbering the first page (like a cover page)?
Many page-numbering tools let you start the visible numbering from a later page while still counting the cover internally. Check the specific option in the tool before applying.
Q.Will adding page numbers affect my document's existing formatting?
No — the number is added as a small overlay in the margin area; the rest of the page content is untouched.
Q.Is it safe to number a document with sensitive content?
Yes — PaperStack adds page numbers entirely inside your browser. Your file is never uploaded to a server.
