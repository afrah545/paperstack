How to Delete Pages from a PDF — Free, No Upload Required

5 min read · Updated June 2026

You've got a PDF, but a few pages need to go — a blank page after scanning, a cover sheet before sharing, a confidential section, or an appendix nobody asked for. The problem is most ways to remove PDF pages either cost money, require installing software, or ask you to upload your file to a server you've never heard of.

PaperStack's Delete PDF Pages tool does it entirely in your browser. Your file never leaves your device — not even for a second.

1. When would you need to delete PDF pages?

It's more common than people expect. Here are the situations that come up most often:

  • Blank pages from scanning — scanners often produce empty pages between sections or at the end of a document.
  • Removing sensitive information — a page with someone's personal data, salary details, or private notes that shouldn't be in the version you share.
  • Cleaning up a report or proposal — dropping the internal cover page, draft watermarks, or appendix pages before sending to a client.
  • Trimming study material — keeping only the chapters you need from a long textbook or lecture PDF.
  • Fixing a merged PDF — when you combine files and end up with duplicate or out-of-place pages.

3. Is it safe? What happens to my file?

This is the question that matters most, especially if your PDF has anything personal in it — a contract, a payslip, an ID scan.

PaperStack processes your file using PDF-lib, a JavaScript library that runs entirely inside your browser tab. When you select a file, it loads into your browser's memory. No data is sent to any server, stored anywhere, or seen by anyone other than you. The moment you close the tab, it's gone.

This makes it meaningfully safer than upload-based PDF tools, where your file goes to a third-party server — even if they claim to delete it after processing.

4. What happens to the rest of the document?

📄 Content and formatting

Every other page stays exactly as it was. Text, images, fonts, annotations — nothing changes except the pages you removed.

🔢 Page numbers

If your PDF has printed page numbers inside the content (not browser-generated ones), those won't auto-update. Use the Page Numbers tool afterwards to re-add clean numbering.

📎 Bookmarks and links

Internal bookmarks that pointed to a deleted page may break. For simple documents — forms, reports, scanned files — this is rarely an issue.

5. Does it work on mobile?

Yes. The tool works on Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge — on desktop and mobile alike. On an iPhone or Android, just open PaperStack in your mobile browser and use it the same way. No app download needed.

Common questions

Q.Can I delete multiple pages at once?
Yes. Select as many pages as you want before hitting "Delete Pages." There's no limit on how many you can remove in one go.

You'll need to remove the password first. Use PaperStack's Unlock PDF tool, then come back and delete the pages you want.

Yes — fewer pages means a smaller file. If you want to reduce it further, run the result through the Compress PDF tool afterwards.
